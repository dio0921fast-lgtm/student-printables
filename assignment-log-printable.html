About this printable

An assignment log printable gives students one running list for schoolwork across classes. Instead of keeping due dates in different notebooks, students can use this page to see what has been assigned, what is urgent, and what has already been turned in.

This assignment log printable is designed for students who want a practical paper worksheet for assignment names, class details, due dates, priority levels, and completion status. It can be used at home, in class, during tutoring, or as part of a binder system. The layout is simple enough to fill in quickly, but structured enough to help students make better decisions about what to do next.

What is included

  • Assignment date column
  • Class and assignment columns
  • Due date tracking
  • Priority and status fields
  • Turned-in checkbox
  • Enough rows for a full week or unit

How to use this template

  1. Add each assignment when it is given.
  2. Fill in the due date before leaving class.
  3. Mark the priority if the task is large or urgent.
  4. Update the status as you work.
  5. Check off the item only after it is turned in.

Best for

  • Middle school students building stronger organization habits.
  • High school students balancing homework, tests, and activities.
  • College students who prefer a quick paper planning tool.
  • Homeschool routines, tutoring sessions, and study support.
  • Teachers and parents who want a simple printable page for student planning.

Example use case

A high school student can use this assignment log during a busy week with English reading, math practice, history questions, and a science lab. The page shows what is due first and which assignments still need to be submitted.

Printing tips

Print this page on US Letter or A4 paper. Choose fit to page in the print window so the worksheet stays inside the margins. Students can keep a copy in a binder, tape it near a desk, or place it in a dry erase sleeve for repeated use. For classroom use, print several copies and let students choose a fresh page when a new week, unit, project, or exam begins.
