About this assignment planner template

An assignment planner template helps students break one assignment into smaller pieces instead of waiting until the due date feels urgent. It is useful for essays, projects, presentations, worksheets, lab reports, reading responses, and any school task that has more than one step.

This printable assignment planner template gives students space to write the assignment name, class, due date, materials, steps, checkpoints, priority, and final submission details. It supports students who need a clear path from “assigned” to “turned in” without relying only on memory or a classroom portal.

This page is intentionally simple so it stays useful after printing. Students can write by hand, teachers can copy it for a class, and parents can keep it in a homework folder or binder.

What is included

  • Assignment details for class, due date, and final goal.

  • Status and done boxes to track progress.
  • Teacher instructions and questions sections.
  • A submission checklist for final review before turning work in.

How to use this template

  1. Write the assignment name, class, final due date, and main goal.
  2. Read the teacher instructions and copy the most important requirements into the planner.
  3. Break the assignment into smaller steps that can be finished one at a time.
  4. Add a due date or target date for each step.
  5. Track the status of each part until the final check and submission are complete.
  6. Use the questions section when you need help from a teacher, tutor, parent, or classmate.

Best for

  • Middle school students building stronger homework and study routines.
  • High school students managing several classes, deadlines, and exams.
  • College students who prefer a printable page beside digital calendars.
  • Homeschool families, tutoring sessions, teachers, and parents.

Example use case

A student has a history presentation due in two weeks. They use the assignment planner template to plan research, source notes, slides, speaking practice, and final review. Each step gets a target date, and the student writes down a question about citation format before asking the teacher. The assignment becomes easier because it is split into visible parts.

The same page can also be reused for tutoring, independent study, class planning, and weekly reset routines. The goal is not to make planning complicated. The goal is to help students see the next action clearly enough to start.

Printing tips

Print on US Letter or A4 paper and choose fit to page before printing. Keep the printable in a binder, clipboard, homework folder, or dry erase sleeve. If the same routine repeats, laminate one copy or place it in a reusable sleeve with a dry erase marker.
