About this daily study schedule template

This daily study schedule template helps students turn one busy day into a realistic plan. It is useful when homework, review, reading, practice questions, and breaks all need a place in the same afternoon or evening.

The printable is more focused than a weekly planner. Students can use it for one school day, one exam prep day, one weekend study block, or one tutoring plan where the next steps need to be clear.

What is included

  • Daily time blocks for school, homework, review, and practice.
  • Subject, study task, materials, notes, and done columns.
  • Priority space for the most important study tasks.
  • A next-step area for tomorrow or the next class.
  • A print-friendly layout for quick handwriting.

How to use this template

  1. Print one daily study schedule template for the day you want to plan.
  2. Write your fixed school or activity times first.
  3. Add homework, review, and practice tasks into realistic blocks.
  4. Include short breaks so the schedule is easier to follow.
  5. Check off each block and write the next step before stopping.

Best for

  • Students planning one focused study day.
  • Exam review, homework catch-up, tutoring, and homeschool routines.
  • High school and college students balancing several tasks after class.
  • Teachers and parents helping students build time management habits.

Example use case

A student can print this daily study schedule template before a busy evening. They block out math homework from 4:00 to 4:40, a break, science review from 5:00 to 5:30, dinner, and English reading after dinner. Instead of guessing what to do next, the student follows the plan one block at a time.

Printing tips

Print on US Letter or A4 paper and choose fit to page. If you use this page often, keep blank copies in a study binder or print one copy for a dry erase sleeve. A clipboard also works well for students who move between home, school, and tutoring.
