About this printable

A homework checklist printable gives students a simple place to write each task, check what is finished, and notice what still needs attention before the next school day. It is helpful when homework comes from several classes and small details are easy to forget.

This homework checklist printable is designed for students who want a practical paper worksheet for homework tasks, due dates, required materials, and completed assignments. It can be used at home, in class, during tutoring, or as part of a binder system. The layout is simple enough to fill in quickly, but structured enough to help students make better decisions about what to do next.

What is included

  • Space to list homework from multiple classes
  • Checkboxes for finished work
  • Columns for due dates and materials
  • Notes area for reminders from teachers
  • A weekly goal line for focus
  • Print-friendly table rows for handwriting

How to use this template

  1. Print one copy at the start of the week.
  2. Write each homework task as soon as it is assigned.
  3. Add due dates and materials so nothing is forgotten.
  4. Check off each item after it is finished and packed.
  5. Review the list before bed or before leaving for school.

Best for

  • Middle school students building stronger organization habits.
  • High school students balancing homework, tests, and activities.
  • College students who prefer a quick paper planning tool.
  • Homeschool routines, tutoring sessions, and study support.
  • Teachers and parents who want a simple printable page for student planning.

Example use case

A student can use this checklist on Monday afternoon to list math problems, a science reading, and an English paragraph. By Thursday, the student can quickly see which tasks are done and which assignment still needs a textbook or worksheet.

Printing tips

Print this page on US Letter or A4 paper. Choose fit to page in the print window so the worksheet stays inside the margins. Students can keep a copy in a binder, tape it near a desk, or place it in a dry erase sleeve for repeated use. For classroom use, print several copies and let students choose a fresh page when a new week, unit, project, or exam begins.
