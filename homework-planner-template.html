About this homework planner template

This homework planner template gives students a simple place to write every homework task before it gets lost in a notebook, chat message, or class handout. It is built for daily and weekly homework routines, especially when a student has several classes with different due dates.

The printable works well beside a weekly planner or assignment tracker. Students can use it after school, during study hall, in tutoring sessions, or at the end of each class period to capture what needs to be finished next.

What is included

  • Homework rows for Monday through Sunday.
  • Subject, task, due date, priority, time, and done columns.
  • Extra space for materials, questions, and reminders.
  • A clean printable table for handwriting.
  • A layout that works for one class or multiple classes.

How to use this template

  1. Print one homework planner template at the start of the week.
  2. Write each homework task as soon as it is assigned.
  3. Add the due date and mark urgent work with a priority note.
  4. Estimate how long each task may take before starting.
  5. Check off finished work and move unfinished tasks to the next day.

Best for

  • Middle school students learning to manage several classes.
  • High school students with homework due on different days.
  • College students who prefer a paper homework plan.
  • Homeschool routines, tutoring folders, teachers, and parents.

Example use case

A student can fill in this homework planner template after school on Monday. They add math practice due Wednesday, a history reading due Thursday, and a science worksheet due Friday. By writing estimated time beside each task, they can decide which homework to finish first and which materials to pack for the next day.

Printing tips

Print on US Letter or A4 paper and choose fit to page before printing. Keep the planner in a binder, homework folder, clipboard, or dry erase sleeve. If the same weekly structure repeats, laminate one copy or use a sleeve with a dry erase marker.
