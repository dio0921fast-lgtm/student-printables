How to Use This Reading Log

  1. Write down each book title after starting a new book or reading assignment.
  2. Track reading dates so students can see when reading happened.
  3. Record pages read after each session, chapter, or assigned reading block.
  4. Add notes, favorite parts, questions, or short reflections while the book is fresh.
  5. Give each book or reading session a rating if students want to remember what they enjoyed.
  6. Review reading progress weekly with a teacher, parent, tutor, or homeschool instructor.

What to Include in a Reading Log

  • Book title
  • Author
  • Date started
  • Date finished
  • Pages read
  • Rating
  • Short summary
  • Notes

Who This Reading Log Is For

  • Elementary students building a regular reading habit.
  • Middle school students tracking independent reading and class novels.
  • High school students recording books, articles, chapters, or assigned texts.
  • Homeschool families documenting reading progress across the year.
  • Teachers assigning reading logs for classroom reading, book clubs, or homework.
  • Parents who want a simple way to see what their child has read.

Reading log ideas for school and homeschool

Students can use this student reading log for independent reading, classroom reading assignments, library challenges, summer reading, book reports, or daily homework. Younger students may write only the book title and pages read, while older students can add ratings, notes, summaries, and favorite quotes.

Teachers can collect the reading log weekly or keep it in a reading folder. Homeschool families can save completed pages as part of a portfolio. Parents can use the log to talk about reading progress without needing a complicated app or spreadsheet.

Printing tips

Print this reading log PDF on US Letter or A4 paper and choose fit to page before printing. Keep copies in a binder, reading folder, classroom station, homeschool notebook, or dry erase sleeve. For repeated use, print one copy and place it in a sleeve so students can reuse it with a dry erase marker.

FAQ

What is a reading log?

A reading log is a simple record of books, authors, reading dates, pages read, ratings, and notes. Students use it to remember what they read and track reading progress over time.

How do students use a reading log?

Students write down each book title, author, date, pages read, rating, and short notes after each reading session. They can review the log weekly to see progress and choose what to read next.
