About this printable

A research planner printable helps students manage a paper, presentation, or project before the work becomes messy. It gives students space to define a research question, track sources, collect useful notes, and plan the next steps toward a finished assignment.

This research planner printable is designed for students who want a practical paper worksheet for research questions, sources, notes, project deadlines, and writing steps. It can be used at home, in class, during tutoring, or as part of a binder system. The layout is simple enough to fill in quickly, but structured enough to help students make better decisions about what to do next.

How to use this template

  1. Write the topic and due date.
  2. Turn the topic into a focused research question.
  3. Record each source as you find it.
  4. Copy only useful evidence or notes.
  5. Mark which sources will be used in the final project.

Best for

  • Middle school students building stronger organization habits.
  • High school students balancing homework, tests, and activities.
  • College students who prefer a quick paper planning tool.
  • Homeschool routines, tutoring sessions, and study support.
  • Teachers and parents who want a simple printable page for student planning.

Example use case

A student writing about renewable energy can use this planner to record articles, note useful statistics, track citation details, and decide which sources support the final essay or presentation.

Printing tips

Print this page on US Letter or A4 paper. Choose fit to page in the print window so the worksheet stays inside the margins. Students can keep a copy in a binder, tape it near a desk, or place it in a dry erase sleeve for repeated use. For classroom use, print several copies and let students choose a fresh page when a new week, unit, project, or exam begins.
