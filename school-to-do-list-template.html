About this school to do list template

This school to do list template helps students get tasks out of their head and onto paper. It is useful for daily school planning, homework nights, tutoring sessions, classroom reminders, and busy weeks when several small tasks are easy to forget.

The printable keeps priorities, due dates, and done boxes close together. Students can use it as a simple morning checklist, an after-school homework list, or a weekly reset page before a new school week starts.

What is included

  • A printable school task list with checkbox rows.
  • Priority and due date columns for quick planning.
  • Space for class names, notes, and reminders.
  • A top-three priority area for the most important work.
  • A clean layout that works for daily or weekly school planning.

How to use this template

  1. Print one copy before school, after school, or at the start of the week.
  2. Write every task in the list before sorting priorities.
  3. Mark the most important tasks in the priority column.
  4. Add due dates and short notes while they are fresh.
  5. Check off finished tasks and move unfinished work to a fresh page.

Best for

  • Students who need a simple daily school checklist.
  • Middle school and high school homework routines.
  • College students who want a quick paper task list.
  • Homeschool, tutoring, teachers, and parent support.

Example use case

A student can use this school to do list template after class to write down homework, a permission slip, a reading assignment, and a quiz review task. They choose the top three tasks, add due dates, and check off items as they finish them before packing their backpack.

Printing tips

Print on US Letter or A4 paper and choose fit to page. Keep a stack near a desk or place one copy in a binder pocket. For repeated daily use, put the page in a dry erase sleeve and rewrite tasks with a dry erase marker.
