About this semester assignment tracker

This semester assignment tracker helps students see important assignments across a full term instead of only one week at a time. It is designed for students who need a longer view of papers, projects, quizzes, presentations, readings, online work, labs, and other graded tasks. The semester assignment tracker printable gives one place to record the course, due date, type, weight, status, and completion check.

A semester view is helpful because some assignments matter more than others. A small homework task and a final project should not feel the same. By writing assignment type and weight, students can make better choices about when to start, what to ask about, and what deserves extra planning time.

What is included

  • Semester-level assignment rows for projects, essays, exams, and class tasks.
  • Course, due date, type, weight, status, and done columns.
  • Space for major deadlines and follow-up notes.
  • A printable assignment tracker for semester planning.

The printable is intentionally plain and easy to write on. Students can use pencil, pen, highlighter, or a dry erase marker if the page is placed in a sleeve. The table area is designed to be useful as a real worksheet, not just a decorative preview.

How to use this template

  1. Print the tracker at the start of a semester or grading period.
  2. Copy major dates from syllabi, class calendars, or online course pages.
  3. Add assignment type and grade weight when that information is available.
  4. Update the status column weekly as tasks move from not started to submitted.
  5. Review the major deadlines box before choosing weekly study priorities.

Students should keep the completed page somewhere visible, such as a binder pocket, desk, clipboard, fridge, classroom folder, or tutoring notebook. A visible paper plan often works better than a hidden digital reminder when a student is trying to build a new habit.

Example use case

A college student can use this semester assignment tracker during the first week of classes. They copy due dates from three syllabi: a research paper, two quizzes, a lab report, a group presentation, and a final exam project. The tracker shows that two large deadlines fall in the same week, so the student can start research earlier and schedule professor questions before the deadline is close.

Students can keep the semester assignment tracker at the front of a binder and pair it with a weekly planner. The semester page shows the big picture, while the weekly page shows what should happen today or this week.

For teachers and homeschool parents, this tracker can also serve as a planning conversation tool. Instead of asking whether school is "fine," adults can point to specific upcoming assignments and ask what support is needed.

Printing tips

Print on US Letter or A4 paper and choose fit to page before printing. For repeated use, keep the sheet in a binder, homework folder, clipboard, or dry erase sleeve. Teachers can print a small stack for classroom routines, while parents and tutors can keep extra copies near a study area. If a student wants a cleaner writing surface, print in black and white and avoid scaling the page larger than the printable area.

For younger students, it can help to fill in the first row together before asking them to complete the rest independently. For older students, the same page can become a quick weekly reset tool that keeps assignments, study time, and progress visible.
