How to choose a printable

Pick the task first

Choose a tracker for homework, a planner for time management, a checklist for preparation, or a schedule for classes and study blocks.

Keep it visible

Printed pages work best when students keep them in a binder, folder, clipboard, or dry erase sleeve where they can check them daily.

Print new copies as needed

Most students use new pages weekly, monthly, before exams, or when a new project or semester begins.

FAQ

Are these student planner printables free?

Yes. These student planner printables are free to print for school, tutoring, homeschool, or personal study planning.

Can I save the pages as PDF files?

Yes. Open any printable, click the print button, and choose Save as PDF in your browser print window.

What paper size works best?

US Letter and A4 both work well. Choose fit to page before printing.

Can teachers use these pages?

Yes. Teachers, tutors, and parents can print these templates for classroom or student organization use.

Where should I start?

Start with a weekly study planner, homework tracker, or student planner if you want one page that helps with several school tasks.
