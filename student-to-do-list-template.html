About this student to do list template

A student to do list template gives students one simple place to collect school tasks before the day gets busy. Instead of keeping homework in one notebook, reminders in another place, and test prep in memory, students can write the full list on a printable page and decide what needs attention first. This student to do list template is designed for school days, homework blocks, tutoring sessions, homeschool routines, and weekly reset time.

The page works for middle school, high school, college, and homeschool students because it keeps the structure clear: task, class, due date, priority, materials, and completion. Students can use it for one busy day, a full week, or a specific class. Parents and teachers can also print copies for students who need a more visible paper routine.

This page is intentionally simple so it stays useful after printing. Students can write by hand, teachers can copy it for a class, and parents can keep it in a homework folder or binder.

What is included

  • Priority sections for urgent, medium, and low tasks.
  • Class and due date columns for school assignments.
  • Materials space so students remember books, devices, or handouts.
  • Done checkboxes that make progress visible.
  • Notes boxes for reminders, questions, and tomorrow’s tasks.
  • A clean printable layout that works in binders and folders.

How to use this template

  1. Print one student to do list template before the school day or at the start of the week.
  2. Write every task in the list before choosing what to do first.
  3. Add the class, due date, and materials needed for each item.
  4. Mark the highest priority tasks so the most important work starts first.
  5. Check off finished tasks and move unfinished items to a fresh list.
  6. Review the notes box before packing your bag or ending the study session.

Best for

  • Middle school students building stronger homework and study routines.
  • High school students managing several classes, deadlines, and exams.
  • College students who prefer a printable page beside digital calendars.
  • Homeschool families, tutoring sessions, teachers, and parents.

Example use case

A student gets home with math homework, a science quiz to review, reading pages, and a permission slip. They write each item on the student to do list template, mark the quiz review as high priority, add the book and calculator under materials, and check tasks off after each study block. By the end of the evening, the unfinished reading can be moved to tomorrow instead of being forgotten.

The same page can also be reused for tutoring, independent study, class planning, and weekly reset routines. The goal is not to make planning complicated. The goal is to help students see the next action clearly enough to start.

Printing tips

Print on US Letter or A4 paper and choose fit to page before printing. Keep the printable in a binder, clipboard, homework folder, or dry erase sleeve. If the same routine repeats, laminate one copy or place it in a reusable sleeve with a dry erase marker.
