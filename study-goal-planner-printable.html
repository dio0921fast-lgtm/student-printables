About this printable

A study goal planner printable helps students turn a broad goal like do better in science into smaller steps that can actually be completed. It is useful before a new unit, grading period, test week, or tutoring session.

This study goal planner printable is designed for students who want a practical paper worksheet for study goals, action steps, deadlines, progress notes, and review habits. It can be used at home, in class, during tutoring, or as part of a binder system. The layout is simple enough to fill in quickly, but structured enough to help students make better decisions about what to do next.

How to use this template

  1. Write one clear study goal.
  2. Explain why the goal matters.
  3. List the notes, textbook pages, videos, or practice sheets needed.
  4. Break the goal into small action steps.
  5. Review progress at the end of each study session.

Best for

  • Middle school students building stronger organization habits.
  • High school students balancing homework, tests, and activities.
  • College students who prefer a quick paper planning tool.
  • Homeschool routines, tutoring sessions, and study support.
  • Teachers and parents who want a simple printable page for student planning.

Example use case

A student preparing for a biology test can set a goal to review cell structure, list flashcards and class notes as resources, then divide the work into vocabulary, diagrams, practice questions, and a final self-quiz.

Printing tips

Print this page on US Letter or A4 paper. Choose fit to page in the print window so the worksheet stays inside the margins. Students can keep a copy in a binder, tape it near a desk, or place it in a dry erase sleeve for repeated use. For classroom use, print several copies and let students choose a fresh page when a new week, unit, project, or exam begins.
