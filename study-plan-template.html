About this study plan template

A study plan template helps students turn a broad goal, such as getting ready for a test or improving a class grade, into smaller steps that can actually fit into a week. This printable is built for students who need to see what to study, when to study it, and how they will know the session is complete.

The study plan template can be used before an exam, during a difficult unit, at the start of a semester, or anytime a student feels behind. It combines goals, subjects, study blocks, resources, practice tasks, and reflection in one printable page so students can plan without opening an app.

This page is intentionally simple so it stays useful after printing. Students can write by hand, teachers can copy it for a class, and parents can keep it in a homework folder or binder.

What is included

  • A clear study goal area for the week or exam period.
  • Daily rows for subjects, topics, tasks, and time blocks.
  • A resource box for notes, textbooks, videos, flashcards, or practice tests.
  • Completion checkboxes for finished study tasks.
  • Progress notes for what worked and what needs more review.
  • A printable structure for school, homeschool, tutoring, and test prep.

How to use this template

  1. Choose one main study goal before filling in the page.
  2. Break the goal into subjects, topics, or skills that need review.
  3. Assign each topic to a realistic day and time block.
  4. Write the study task clearly, such as reread notes, make flashcards, or complete practice questions.
  5. Check off completed sessions and write what still feels confusing.
  6. Print a new study plan template when the unit, exam, or weekly goal changes.

Best for

  • Middle school students building stronger homework and study routines.
  • High school students managing several classes, deadlines, and exams.
  • College students who prefer a printable page beside digital calendars.
  • Homeschool families, tutoring sessions, teachers, and parents.

Example use case

A high school student has a biology test on Friday. They use the study plan template to list cell vocabulary, diagrams, textbook review, and practice questions. Monday is for notes, Tuesday is for diagrams, Wednesday is for flashcards, and Thursday is for practice questions. The progress notes show which topics still need help before the test.

The same page can also be reused for tutoring, independent study, class planning, and weekly reset routines. The goal is not to make planning complicated. The goal is to help students see the next action clearly enough to start.

Printing tips

Print on US Letter or A4 paper and choose fit to page before printing. Keep the printable in a binder, clipboard, homework folder, or dry erase sleeve. If the same routine repeats, laminate one copy or place it in a reusable sleeve with a dry erase marker.
