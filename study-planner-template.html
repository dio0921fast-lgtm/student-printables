About this study planner template

A study planner template gives students a practical weekly view of schoolwork, study sessions, homework, and review tasks. It is more focused than a general calendar because every section is built around studying: what needs to be learned, which tasks matter most, and what still needs attention.

This printable study planner template is useful for students who want a paper plan they can keep beside a laptop, inside a binder, or on a desk. It supports regular homework routines, exam preparation, class projects, tutoring goals, and homeschool lessons without adding complicated tracking systems.

This page is intentionally simple so it stays useful after printing. Students can write by hand, teachers can copy it for a class, and parents can keep it in a homework folder or binder.

How to use this template

  1. Print the study planner template at the start of the week.
  2. Write one study goal for each day or class.
  3. Add homework, review tasks, and practice work beside each goal.
  4. Use the priority column to mark work that needs to happen first.
  5. Check off completed sessions and write notes about what needs more review.
  6. Keep the finished planner in a binder to see patterns across several weeks.

Best for

  • High school students managing several classes, deadlines, and exams.
  • College students who prefer a printable page beside digital calendars.

Example use case

A student with English, math, and history assignments prints the study planner template on Sunday night. They add math practice on Monday, English reading on Tuesday, history review on Wednesday, and a catch-up block on Saturday. The priority section helps them finish the assignment due first without ignoring longer-term review.

The same page can also be reused for tutoring, independent study, class planning, and weekly reset routines. The goal is not to make planning complicated. The goal is to help students see the next action clearly enough to start.

For students who feel overwhelmed by several classes, this printable also works as a weekly reset page. It gives them a quiet place to collect loose tasks, choose realistic study blocks, and decide what can wait until later.

Printing tips

Print on US Letter or A4 paper and choose fit to page before printing. Keep the printable in a binder, clipboard, homework folder, or dry erase sleeve. If the same routine repeats, laminate one copy or place it in a reusable sleeve with a dry erase marker.
