About this weekly assignment planner

This weekly assignment planner gives students a clear place to organize every assignment that appears during a school week. Instead of keeping tasks in separate notebooks, online portals, email reminders, and loose papers, students can write each assignment in one weekly view. The layout works as a weekly assignment planner printable for homework, essays, projects, readings, quizzes, and classroom tasks that need a due date and a next step.

A weekly assignment planner is especially useful when several classes assign work at the same time. Students can see what is due soon, what can wait, and which assignment needs a teacher question or extra material. Parents, tutors, and homeschool families can use the page during check-ins because it makes the week visible without needing to open a school account or learning platform.

What is included

  • A Monday through Sunday assignment planning table.
  • Columns for class, assignment, due date, priority, next step, and done status.
  • Extra space for assignments due soon and materials or questions.
  • A printable worksheet layout that fits homework folders and binders.

The printable is intentionally plain and easy to write on. Students can use pencil, pen, highlighter, or a dry erase marker if the page is placed in a sleeve. The table area is designed to be useful as a real worksheet, not just a decorative preview.

How to use this template

  1. Print one copy at the beginning of the school week.
  2. Write each assignment as soon as it is given in class or posted online.
  3. Add the due date, priority, and the next physical step needed to start.
  4. Review the planner each afternoon before beginning homework.
  5. Move unfinished assignments to the carry over row or to next week.

Students should keep the completed page somewhere visible, such as a binder pocket, desk, clipboard, fridge, classroom folder, or tutoring notebook. A visible paper plan often works better than a hidden digital reminder when a student is trying to build a new habit.

Example use case

A student can fill out this weekly assignment planner on Monday after school. They add a math worksheet due Wednesday, a history reading due Thursday, a biology lab question due Friday, and an English paragraph that needs a rough draft. The planner helps them notice that two assignments need textbook pages, one needs a calculator, and one should be started early because it requires writing. By Friday, completed tasks are checked off and unfinished work is moved to the carry over row for the weekend.

For students who tend to forget small assignments, the most helpful habit is writing the next action instead of only the assignment name. For example, write "open rubric and choose topic" instead of only "essay." That small detail makes the page easier to use when a student is tired after school.

Teachers can also use this assignment planner template as a weekly planning handout. It works well for advisory classes, study skills lessons, tutoring folders, and homework intervention because it keeps the plan simple and visible.

Printing tips

Print on US Letter or A4 paper and choose fit to page before printing. For repeated use, keep the sheet in a binder, homework folder, clipboard, or dry erase sleeve. Teachers can print a small stack for classroom routines, while parents and tutors can keep extra copies near a study area. If a student wants a cleaner writing surface, print in black and white and avoid scaling the page larger than the printable area.

For younger students, it can help to fill in the first row together before asking them to complete the rest independently. For older students, the same page can become a quick weekly reset tool that keeps assignments, study time, and progress visible.
