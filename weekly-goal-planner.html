About this printable

A weekly goal planner helps students turn a vague school goal into smaller actions. It works well for homework routines, exam preparation, reading goals, grade improvement, and project progress.

This weekly goal planner gives students space to choose priorities, plan action steps, mark days to work, and reflect at the end of the week. It is simple enough for a binder and structured enough for tutoring or homeschool planning.

What is included

  • Progress and done columns

How to use this template

  1. Choose one main goal for the week.
  2. Break the goal into small action steps.
  3. Mark which days you will work on each step.
  4. Review progress midweek and adjust if needed.
  5. Use the reflection box to plan next week.

Best for

  • Middle school students who need clear school organization.
  • High school students balancing assignments, exams, reading, and activities.
  • College students who prefer simple printable planning pages.
  • Homeschool routines, tutoring sessions, and independent study.
  • Teachers and parents who want a practical paper worksheet for student support.

Example use case

A student who wants to raise a science grade can set a weekly goal to finish missing notes, review vocabulary, and ask one question in class. The planner shows exactly when each step will happen.

Printing tips

Print this page on US Letter or A4 paper. Choose fit to page in the print window so the worksheet stays inside the margins. Students can keep a copy in a binder, tape it near a desk, or place it in a dry erase sleeve for repeated use. For classroom use, print several copies and let students choose a fresh page when a new week, unit, project, or exam begins.
