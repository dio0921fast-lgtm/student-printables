About this weekly homework planner template

This weekly homework planner template helps students see every homework task for the week in one calm, printable place. It is useful when assignments come from several classes and students need to remember due dates, materials, priorities, and completion status.

Students can use the page at the start of the week, after school each day, or during a tutoring session. The weekly layout keeps homework visible from Monday through Sunday, so unfinished work can be moved before it becomes stressful.

What is included

  • Monday through Sunday homework planning rows.
  • Subject, task, due date, priority, time, and done columns.
  • Extra space for materials and teacher questions.
  • Printable checkbox column for finished work.
  • A weekly view that works for one class or several classes.

How to use this template

  1. Print one copy at the beginning of the week.
  2. Add homework as soon as each task is assigned.
  3. Write the due date and estimate how long the task may take.
  4. Mark urgent or difficult work with a priority note.
  5. Check off finished homework and move unfinished work forward.

Best for

  • Middle school students learning weekly routines.
  • High school students balancing homework from several classes.
  • College students who want a paper homework overview.
  • Homeschool, tutoring, teachers, and parents.

Example use case

A student can fill in this weekly homework planner template on Monday after school. They write math practice due Wednesday, history reading due Thursday, and a science worksheet due Friday. By checking the priority and time columns, they can decide which homework should be finished first and what can wait until the weekend.

Printing tips

Print on US Letter or A4 paper and choose fit to page before printing. Keep the page in a binder, homework folder, clipboard, or dry erase sleeve. If the same routine repeats, laminate one copy or use a sleeve with a dry erase marker.
